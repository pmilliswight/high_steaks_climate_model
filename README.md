# High Steaks: U.S. Beef Industry Climate Impact Modeling

Co-authored peer-reviewed research estimating the marginal long-term warming 
attributable to the U.S. beef cattle system through year 2500, using a 
reduced-complexity climate model coupled to a regional beef herd model.

**Key finding:** N2O (nitrous oxide), not methane, dominates long-term 
temperature impact from beef, comprising ~70% of warming at the 500-year 
timescale. This contradicts the conventional CO2-equivalent metric, which 
over-emphasizes short-lived methane emissions.

**My contribution:** Emissions modeling, data integration, and analysis 
of upstream feed production emission pathways across seven U.S. regions.

**Tools:** Python, differential equations modeling, RCP scenario data, USDA cattle data  
**Methods:** Reduced-complexity climate modeling, ODE systems, emissions accounting  
**Program:** NSF-funded QRLSSP Summer Program, ASU (Summer 2024)
